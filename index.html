The problem

Most of the web still wasn't built for everyone.

Tiny click targets, dense layouts, low contrast, and interfaces that assume one way to read, navigate, and interact. Millions of people run into these barriers every day.

Adaptive browsing should not mean fighting the page. Axol Assist exists to build software that helps people adjust the web to fit how they actually use it, whether through the browser or on the sites they visit.

Who it's for

When the default web doesn't fit.

Anyone whose body, vision, focus, or energy makes standard interfaces harder than they should be.

Mobility & pain

Tremors, RSI, chronic pain, or limited reach. Tools that reduce clicking strain and enlarge targets.

Vision & reading

Low vision, dyslexia, and focus challenges. Adjust type, spacing, contrast, and reading layout on any page.

Alternative input

Eye gaze, head pointers, joysticks, and screen fatigue. Browse with less precision and less exhaustion.

Our mission

Why Axol Assist exists.

Accessibility tools are often expensive, buried in browser settings, or built for compliance checklists instead of daily browsing. People who need help the most are left searching for workarounds.

We build practical software that meets people where they already are: in the browser, on the sites they visit, without new hardware or enterprise contracts. Our goal is simple: help more people browse comfortably, read clearly, and interact with less strain.
