The problem

Most websites weren't built with everyone in mind.

Tiny click targets. Gray text on gray backgrounds. Dense pages with nowhere for your eyes to rest. Expensive overlays that hide behind a paywall. Most accessibility products assume you will change how you work to fit the tool. We think it should be the opposite. Software should meet people where they already are: in the browser, on the sites they use every day.

Who it's for

When the default web doesn't fit.

Accessibility is not a niche. It is anyone whose body, vision, focus, or energy level makes the standard interface harder than it should be.

Mobility, pain & repetitive strain

Tremors, chronic pain, RSI, spinal cord injury, MS, ALS, a wrist in a cast. When every click costs effort, hover-to-click and larger targets can mean getting through your day without burning out.

Low vision, dyslexia & focus

Blurry type, harsh contrast, lines that swim on the page, tabs that won't stay still. Adjust fonts, spacing, and contrast on any site, or strip a page down to what you actually came to read.

Eye tracking, head pointers & fatigue

Eye gaze, head trackers, mouth sticks, joysticks, and plain old screen fatigue after hours online. If moving a cursor is easier than clicking one, or reading one more dense page feels impossible, you're who we build for.

Products

Two products. One standard.

Practical accessibility tools you can install today. No enterprise sales call required.

Free Chrome extension

Accessibility Surfer

Personal browsing controls for any site you visit.

Navigate with less clicking. Read with less strain. Adjust text, spacing, and contrast on any page, use hover-to-click when buttons are hard to hit, or open reading mode when a page is too dense. Works with a mouse, trackpad, head tracker, or eye gaze device.

  • Dwell clicking & pointer assistance
  • Text, font, and contrast controls
  • Reading mode for articles

Script or npm

AccessFlow

Visitor-controlled accessibility for your website.

One script tag or npm import adds a floating toolbar with font, contrast, spacing, and motion controls. Settings persist per visitor in the browser. Install in under a minute on React, Next.js, Astro, Vite, or plain HTML.

  • 15+ visitor-facing controls
  • CDN, npm, or React component
  • No backend or account needed

Why Axol Assist exists

Software that adapts to people.

Accessibility tools should be practical, not performative. Too many solutions are expensive, buried in settings, or hidden behind overlays that site owners never enable. Real users are left to fight the default web on their own.

Axol Assist exists to build software that meets people where they are. We ship browser tools for individuals and embeddable tools for site owners. We test with real users, keep data in the browser, and charge fairly so more people can actually use what we make.

Our commitment: build accessibility technology that is usable on day one, respectful of privacy, and honest about what it can and cannot fix.
